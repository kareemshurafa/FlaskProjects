Asthma is a chronic respiratory disease affecting individuals of all ages. It is caused by inflation and muscle tightening in the airways, making it hard to breathe. Symptoms vary in severity. Asthma can be a serious condition, it can be managed with the right treatment and medication.

Symptoms
Common symptoms include:
•persistent cough, especially at night
•wheezing when exhaling and sometimes inhaling
•shortness of breathe and difficult in breathing
•chest tightness
Causes
Asthma risk factors include family history, other allergic conditions, urban living, and early life events like low birth weight or exposure to pollutants and tobacco smoke. Environmental allergens and irritants, obesity, and certain occupational exposures also increase asthma risk.


WHAT DO WE DO?

ease everyday life of asthma sufferers and raise awareness of the disease.

•allows people with asthma to log their use of their reliever (inhaler), prompt when they need to
